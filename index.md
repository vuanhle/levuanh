## Research Interests

My research focuses on designing **safe and constrained generative models**, generative AI systems that respect hard constraints (e.g., physical laws, safety boundaries, resource limits) and produce verifiably reliable outputs. Unlike standard generative models that optimize solely for likelihood or sample quality, I aim to embed domain-specific constraints directly into the generative process, ensuring that every generated sample is feasible, safe, and useful.

**Key directions:**

- **Constrained generation under physical and logical rules**  
  Integrating ordinary/partial differential equations, conservation laws, and inequality constraints (e.g., stress limits, energy budgets) into the architecture or sampling procedure of VAEs, GANs, diffusion models, and LLM-based generators.

- **Safety guarantees for generative models**  
  Developing methods with provable bounds on constraint violation, robust to distribution shift, and capable of rejecting unsafe outputs. Topics include adversarial robustness, conformal prediction for generative models, and certification of neural generators.

- **Applications across mathematics, science, and engineering**  
  
**Why this matters?**  
Standard generative AI excels at producing plausible outputs, but often fails when those outputs must be *provably* safe or physically realizable. As generative models are deployed in high-stakes settings (autonomous systems, drug design, infrastructure planning), the ability to hard‑wire constraints becomes essential. My work bridges the gap between expressive generative modeling and rigorous constraint satisfaction—enabling AI that is both creative and trustworthy.
